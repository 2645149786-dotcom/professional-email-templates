## 📖 Quick Example

### Asking for a Raise

**❌ What most people write:**

```
Hi [Manager],

I've been here for 2 years now and I think I deserve a raise. 
I work really hard and I've taken on a lot of extra stuff that 
wasn't in my original job description. Also the cost of living 
has gone up a lot. Can we talk about this?

Thanks
```

**✅ What you should write:**

```
Subject: Compensation Discussion — [Your Name]

Hi [Manager],

I'd like to discuss a compensation adjustment based on my 
expanded role and contributions over the past 18 months.

Since starting as [Original Title], my responsibilities have 
grown to include:
- [Responsibility 1] — resulting in [measurable outcome]
- [Responsibility 2] — which previously required [external resource]
- [Responsibility 3] — contributing to [team/company goal]

Based on market data from Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and Payscale, 
the current range for this scope of work is $X–$Y in our market. 
I'm proposing an adjustment to $Z, which reflects the midpoint.

I'd welcome the chance to discuss this in our next 1:1. I've 
documented my contributions in more detail and am happy to share.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

**🧠 Why it works:**
- **Evidence over emotion** — market data + specific contributions vs. "I work really hard"
- **Specific number** — gives the manager something concrete to work with
- **Forwarding-ready** — your manager can send this to HR as-is
- **No ultimatums** — professional tone that keeps the door open

## 🧪 The 5 Rules of Professional Emails

Based on analyzing thousands of email rewrites at [RewriteEmail.com](https://rewriteemail.com):

1. **Delete your first sentence** — Your real opener is almost always sentence #2
2. **One ask per email** — Multiple requests = no action taken
3. **Apologize once, maximum** — Over-apologizing kills credibility
4. **Add specifics everywhere** — Dates, numbers, names beat vague language every time
5. **End with a binary question** — "Does Tuesday work?" > "Let me know your thoughts"
